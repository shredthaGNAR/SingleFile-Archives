Drinking acetone can have several effects on the body, ranging from mild to severe, depending on the amount ingested. Acetone is a colorless, volatile, and flammable liquid with a sweet, pungent odor, and it is commonly used as a solvent in various industries. Here's what can happen if you drink acetone:

  1. Mild Ingestion:

    • Irritation: Small amounts of acetone can cause irritation of the mouth, throat, and stomach.
    • Nausea and Vomiting: Ingesting acetone can lead to feelings of nausea and may induce vomiting.
    • Dizziness: Some people may experience dizziness or lightheadedness.

  2. Moderate Ingestion:

    • Abdominal Pain: Larger amounts can cause abdominal pain and cramping.
    • Diarrhea: Acetone can act as a laxative, leading to diarrhea.
    • Central Nervous System Effects: Moderate ingestion can affect the central nervous system, causing symptoms such as confusion, drowsiness, and even coma in severe cases.

  3. Severe Ingestion:

    • Respiratory Depression: High doses of acetone can lead to respiratory depression, where breathing becomes slow and shallow.
    • Cardiovascular Effects: Severe ingestion can affect the heart, leading to irregular heartbeats (arrhythmias) and even cardiac arrest.
    • Kidney and Liver Damage: Prolonged or severe exposure to acetone can cause damage to the kidneys and liver.
    • Metabolic Acidosis: Acetone ingestion can disrupt the body's acid-base balance, leading to metabolic acidosis, a condition where the body becomes too acidic.

  4. Long-Term Effects:

    • Organ Damage: Chronic ingestion of acetone can lead to long-term damage to the kidneys, liver, and nervous system.
    • Nerve Damage: Prolonged exposure to acetone can cause peripheral neuropathy, leading to numbness, tingling, and weakness in the extremities.

  5. Interactions with Alcohol:

    • Enhanced Effects: Acetone can interact with alcohol, enhancing its depressive effects on the central nervous system. This can lead to increased drowsiness, confusion, and impaired coordination.

If you or someone else has ingested acetone, it is important to seek medical attention, especially if the amount ingested is unknown or if symptoms are severe. Treatment may include supportive care, such as intravenous fluids, and in some cases, gastric lavage (stomach pumping) if done soon after ingestion.
