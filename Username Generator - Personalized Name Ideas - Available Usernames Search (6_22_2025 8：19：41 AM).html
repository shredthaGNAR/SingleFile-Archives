What is a secure username?

A secure username is slightly different from a random username (but is still generated the same way). It should not link you to online or social media accounts.

A secure username does not contain any personally identifiable information, like your first and last name, location, or even date or year of birth that hackers could trace back to your real-life identity.

How often should I change my username?

Unlike your password, you don't need to regularly change the username of any given account. Most online portals, platforms, or logins won't even let you without contacting customer support. So, to avoid this, always use different usernames for each new online account you create for maximum security.

What are the advantages of using a unique username?

There are two main advantages for using unique and secure usernames:

  • anonymity
  • security

Most of us wish to remain anonymous online whilst using social media. Still, we communicate with our family, friends, and colleagues. Using your full name as your username means that those who know you can find you quickly by searching for you.

For instance, if someone searches for you on TikTok or Instagram, the social media platforms return your profile name and your username as results if they are the same. However, with a randomly generated, unidentifiable username, it would be almost impossible to find your profile, even if they sift through your friend's followers too.
